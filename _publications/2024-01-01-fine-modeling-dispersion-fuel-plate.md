For Double Heterogeneous(DH) systems containing particle-dispersed fuel and particle-dispersed burnable poison, the Monte Carlo-based explicit modeling method is often used as a benchmark solution. However, a single fuel plate often contains a large number of particles, and the computational resources required for explicit modeling are enormous. In this paper, a Lattice Modeling Method with Multiple−Particles Universe(LMMMU) is proposed to address this.

To validate the effectiveness of the LMMMU, a variety of particle models for plate fuel were constructed, the particle models of plate fuel were constructed by different packing methods respectively, for the same packing method with stochasticity, 60 models were constructed for exploring the distribution of the calculated results of the different packing methods.

In our work, the differences between four lattice method are compared using the mean value of the stochastic explicit modeling method as a benchmark: regular lattice modeling method with Single- Particle universe(LMMSU), random LMMSU, lattice modeling method with Multi-Particle universe(LMMMU), unrotating LMMMU. Numerical results show that the lattice method can effectively improve computational efficiency and reduce memory consumption. The LMMSU is the most efficient in terms of computational efficiency and consumes the least amount of memory, followed by the multi-particle lattice method. In terms of computational accuracy, the lattice methods all perform well for fuel particles, but for poison particles, the accuracy of LMMMU is higher than that of LMMSU. Comprehensive analysis shows that the LMMMU is better than LMMSU in terms of applicability, comprehensive efficiency, and computational accuracy.
