This paper first employs the traditional RPT (Reactivity-Equivalent Physical Transformation) method to treat fuel plate containing dispersed particle poison. Using the explicit particle model as the benchmark, the impact of this method on neutronic parameters was analyzed. It was found that while the traditional RPT method can achieve reactivity equivalence on a single plate, its application to assembly-level systems results in significant reactivity deviations (up to −525 pcm) and introduces substantial perturbations to power distributions (maximum value exceeds 19 %). 

To address this issue, this study proposes a novel SRPT (Stratified RPT) method applicable to particle poison materials in plate-type fuel elements. Through computational analysis of a specified benchmark case, the transformation ranges of the traditional RPT method and the SRPT method with varying layering configurations were first investigated to determine an optimal layering strategy for the SRPT method. Subsequently, a comparative analysis was conducted on neutronic parameters including energy spectrum, power distribution, and thermal neutron flux across different RPT variants. Finally, the depletion accuracy of the SRPT method was validated. 

Numerical results demonstrate that the SRPT method not only ensures reactivity conservation (deviations < 70 pcm) but also effectively mitigates perturbations in neutronic deviations such as power distribution (70–95 % less than traditional RPT method).
